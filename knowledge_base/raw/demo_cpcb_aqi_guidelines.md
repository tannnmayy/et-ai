# CPCB National Air Quality Index: Categories and Health Advisory

**Organization:** Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB)
**Date:** 2023
**Source:** https://cpcb.nic.in/National-Air-Quality-Index/

## AQI Categories and Breakpoints

The National Air Quality Index (NAQI) developed by CPCB categories measured pollutant concentrations into six categories, each with associated health impacts and advisory messaging.

### Good (0–50 PM2.5: 0–30 µg/m³)
Minimal impact. Normal activities recommended.

### Satisfactory (51–100 PM2.5: 31–60 µg/m³)
May cause minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people. Sensitive individuals should monitor for symptoms.

### Moderate (101–200 PM2.5: 61–90 µg/m³)
May cause breathing discomfort to people with lung disease, heart disease, children, and older adults. Sensitive groups should reduce prolonged outdoor exertion.

### Poor (201–300 PM2.5: 91–120 µg/m³)
May cause breathing discomfort to people on prolonged exposure and discomfort to people with heart or lung disease. Children, elderly, and people with respiratory or cardiac conditions should reduce prolonged outdoor exertion. Schools should consider moving intense outdoor activity indoors.

### Very Poor (301–400 PM2.5: 121–250 µg/m³)
May cause respiratory illness to people on prolonged exposure. Avoid prolonged outdoor exertion for everyone. Sensitive groups should remain indoors where possible.

### Severe (401–500 PM2.5: >250 µg/m³)
May cause respiratory effects even in healthy people and serious health impacts in people with lung or heart disease. Avoid all outdoor exertion. Emergency-level air quality.

## Health Advisories by AQI Category

CPCB recommends that state pollution control boards issue public health advisories when AQI reaches Poor or above. Advisories should target sensitive groups including children, elderly, and people with pre-existing respiratory or cardiac conditions.

## Source Attribution Disclaimer

While AQI categories indicate likely pollution levels, they do not identify specific emission sources. Source apportionment requires additional monitoring and dispersion modelling studies beyond routine AQI measurement.
