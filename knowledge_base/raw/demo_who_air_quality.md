# WHO Global Air Quality Guidelines: Particulate Matter

**Organization:** World Health Organization
**Date:** September 22, 2021
**Source:** https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240034228

## PM2.5 Guidelines

The WHO air quality guidelines (AQG) provide recommended limits for PM2.5 concentrations based on systematic review of health evidence.

### Annual Mean Guideline
Annual mean PM2.5 should not exceed 5 µg/m³.

### 24-Hour Mean Guideline
24-hour mean PM2.5 should not exceed 15 µg/m³ for no more than 3–4 days per year.

## Health Effects of PM2.5 Exposure

Short-term exposure to elevated PM2.5 is associated with:
- Increased daily mortality
- Exacerbation of respiratory and cardiovascular disease
- Increased hospital admissions for respiratory conditions
- Worsening of asthma symptoms

Long-term exposure is associated with:
- Reduced life expectancy
- Increased cardiovascular mortality
- Development of chronic respiratory conditions
- Adverse birth outcomes

## Sensitive Populations

Children, older adults, and individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular or respiratory disease are at increased risk from PM2.5 exposure. These groups should take additional precautions during high-pollution episodes.

## Recommended Actions During High Pollution

During episodes where PM2.5 exceeds guideline levels, WHO recommends:
- Reducing physical activity outdoors, especially prolonged or strenuous activities
- Sensitive individuals should remain indoors where possible
- Using clean indoor air sources if available
- Following national and local authority guidance
