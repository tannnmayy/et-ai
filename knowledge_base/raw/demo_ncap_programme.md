# National Clean Air Programme (NCAP): City Action Plan Guidelines

**Organization:** Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India
**Date:** 2024
**Source:** https://moef.gov.in/ncap/

## Programme Overview

The National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) is a national-level strategy aiming to reduce particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) concentration by 40% by 2025-26 compared to 2017-18 levels, with 2017 as the base year.

## Key Action Areas for City Action Plans

### Industrial Emissions
Cities with industrial zones should implement:
- Regular compliance inspections of industrial emission control systems
- Monitoring of fugitive dust from industrial processes
- Enforcement of emission standards for industrial stacks
- Promotion of cleaner fuel switching

### Vehicular Emissions
Cities should address traffic-related pollution through:
- Strengthening vehicle emission testing and enforcement
- Promoting public transport and non-motorized transport
- Traffic management to reduce congestion and idling
- Control of road dust through mechanical sweeping and water sprinkling

### Construction and Demolition Dust
Action plans must include:
- Enforcement of dust control measures at construction sites
- Covering of construction materials and debris
- Water sprinkling at demolition sites
- Compliance with CPCB construction dust guidelines

### Monitoring and Compliance
Cities should establish:
- Continuous ambient air quality monitoring stations
- Source apportionment studies to identify local emission sources
- Public grievance redressal mechanisms for air quality complaints
- Quarterly review of action plan implementation

## Inspection and Enforcement Framework

State pollution control boards are responsible for conducting inspections of industrial, construction, and other pollution sources. Prioritization should be based on:
- Emission potential and compliance history
- Proximity to sensitive receptors (schools, hospitals, residential areas)
- Contribution to city-level PM concentrations
- Public complaints and reported violations
